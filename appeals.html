Appeals

Ursula uses multiple rhetorical devices to manipulate Ariel such as, appeals to ethos (credibility) - "That's what I do. It's what I live for." - this establishes her credibility as someone who is 'trustworthy' and knows what they are doing. Also, Ursula uses appeals to pathos (emotion/desire) - "This one longing to be thinner, that one wants to get the girl." - this appeals to Ariel's desure and triggers emotions that she can relate to. Lastly, Ursula uses appeals to logos (contracts) - "If you want to cross the bridge, you've got to pay the toll." - she presents the deal/contract logically in order to manipulate Ariel and persuade the kingdom. Ursula's appeals are effective yet cunning.



Through her usage of credibility, emotional manipulation tactics, and logical framing, Ursula poses her offer as something irresistible. By combining a variety of rhetorical appeals she reveals her villainous intent and genius.
