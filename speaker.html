Speaker

The speaker is Ursula, the cunning sea witch who disguises her greed and deception under the narrative of false sympathy. She presents herself as a misunderstood and judged "helper" - the only one who is willingly to assist those who find themselves in need.



"My dear, sweet child, that's what i do. It's what I live for. To help unfortunate merfolk like yourself." This shows how Ursula positions herself as a savior in order to gain Ariel's trust, her tone and phrasing presents her as a caring being - almost like a motherly figure who wants the best for their child, which forms a false sense of security.
